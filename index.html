Climate Change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns, mainly caused by human activities such as burning fossil fuels and deforestation. These changes are already affecting the planet through rising global temperatures, melting ice caps, and more extreme weather events. Understanding climate change is essential for protecting our environment, health, and future generations.

Rising Global Temperatures

The Earth's surface temperature has risen significantly over the past century, mainly due to increased emissions of greenhouse gases like CO₂, CH₄, and N₂O from burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial activities.

Key Impacts:

  • Heatwaves becoming more frequent and intense, posing health risks
  • Prolonged droughts affecting agriculture and water supplies
  • More severe wildfires destroying ecosystems and communities

Melting Ice & Rising Seas

The polar ice caps and glaciers are melting at an alarming rate due to rising temperatures, contributing to rising sea levels and disrupting ecosystems worldwide.

Key Impacts:

  • Coastal flooding and erosion threatening communities
  • Loss of habitat for polar species like bears and seals
  • Disruption of ocean currents and weather patterns

Disruption of Weather Patterns

Climate change alters atmospheric circulation, which influences the jet stream and ocean currents. This causes changes in precipitation patterns, storm intensity, and seasonal behavior.

Key Impacts:

  • Stronger hurricanes and typhoons due to warmer ocean water providing more energy
  • Unpredictable rainfall patterns leading to both floods and droughts
  • Shifting seasons affecting agriculture and natural ecosystems
